### Thoughts of the Week

**Shanghai is Shanghai, Tianjin is Tianjin** Sporadic outbreaks have appeared in various places. Without unified leadership, fighting the epidemic has become a test of government affairs level. Similarly, the epidemic announcement in Shanghai, compared with Tianjin, is higher by an order of magnitude. It is difficult to measure by the number of words in the announcement, but the level is immediately apparent. The higher the level of governance, the more pragmatic.

**The difference between fundamentalism and Buddhism** I often think about where the essential difference between extreme religion and Buddhism lies? Why are some believers so extreme, and even cults appear. But I couldn't figure it out. I accidentally heard Teacher Yu Qiuyu's analysis of this problem in a speech, and it hit the nail on the head. Fundamentalism believes that being one foot away from the doctrine is evil, while Buddhism believes that being one foot away from evil is good.

**Global Thinking** Learning from Israelis and doing global business. It mentions that Israelis rely on innovation and strive to do global business when their domestic market is almost zero. A small company of a dozen people can sell products to dozens of countries around the world. Listening to Teacher Zhou Qiren talk about "Globalization is a ready-made path for enterprises to improve quality", he summarized: Global corporate giants such as BASF and Nestlé, although living in small European cities, rely on innate global strategies, superior quality, precise positioning, and brand building to grow into global giants. I deeply agree. The path of globalization for domestic enterprises that have grown up relying on the huge domestic demand market is rarely mentioned. Especially the reaction of leading enterprises such as Huawei, ZTE, and TikTok when facing economic suppression, which is indeed worrying. However, among all Chinese enterprises, Haier may be the best enterprise in globalization strategy and is worth learning from. This is inseparable from Haier leader Zhang Ruimin. Wu Xiaobo told a story, saying that he visited Zhang Ruimin's small library. Zhang Ruimin told him that I not only read a lot of management books, absorb and learn others' management philosophy, but also can point out others' mistakes. Personally, I think this is the secret of Haier growing into a multinational enterprise.

### Market of the Week

Period: 2020-11-16——2020-11-22

Major indices were mixed this week, but the overall trend remained positive. Yellow rice wine, bicycles, and titanium dioxide led the gains, while lithium batteries, CAR-T and other concepts led the declines. Shanghai Stock Connect saw net inflows for three consecutive weeks, and Hong Kong Stock Connect saw net inflows for four consecutive weeks. Combined with the index and northbound capital flow, funds are flowing to the Shanghai market with smaller gains in the early period, which is also the reason or manifestation of institutions shouting to buy pro-cycle.
