### Weekly Thoughts

Although A-shares have been volatile for two consecutive months in August and September, CITIC Securities, with the rhythm of one research report per week, is firmly bullish on A-shares. The logic for being bullish is: first, a second outbreak of the epidemic is a small probability event; second, the US election, trade war risks are temporarily released; third, short-term market liquidity does not decrease; fourth, full participation in the 14th Five-Year Plan, the future is promising. I still believe in CITIC Securities. Leaving aside strategy, being long on China is being long on one's own life.

### Weekly Events

This week has been incredibly busy (really busy), and I haven't paid much attention to the news. However, there are two eye-catching items in the venture capital circle: first, Luo Zhenyu's Dedao is going for an IPO on the GEM; second, the top venture capital firm Fortune Capital welcomed a milestone moment: the birth of its 100th listed company. With the improvement of the secondary market trading mechanism and IPO rules, listing has become an important channel for venture capital exit, and the linkage between the primary and secondary markets is becoming more and more mature. The entrepreneurial atmosphere will become stronger and stronger.
