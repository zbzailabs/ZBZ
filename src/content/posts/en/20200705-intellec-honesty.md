### Weekly Thoughts

There is a popular saying, "abject poverty in June, turnaround in July". Entering July, driven by blue chips, liquor, securities firms, and real estate, the Shanghai Composite Index broke through 3100 in one fell swoop, and the ChiNext Index broke through 2400. Sell-side institutions unanimously judged that the bull market has arrived, and some exaggerated private equity funds even plausibly claimed that their institution had predicted at the end of 2018 that this round of bull market would start in early 2019, and now it has entered a period of rapid index rise.

As a retail investor (Leek), I also yearn for a bull market, hoping to ride the east wind of the bull market and make a small profit. So, the question is, if the bull market comes, what should we buy?

First, buy the sectors that rise in every bull market. The rise of the bull market is accompanied by the amplification of trading volume and turnover rate, and the profits of securities firms are bound to do their part.

In fact, buying index constituent stocks, the index is an indicator of whether the bull market is coming. Only when the Shanghai, Shenzhen, and ChiNext indices rise sharply does it mean that the bull market has arrived. At the same time, the rise of the bull market is mainly due to the sharp rise in the stock prices of these mainstream index constituent stocks. Therefore, you can buy broad-based indices such as CSI 300 and CSI 500.

Again, buy the hot concepts of this round of bull market. In every round, there are individual sectors whose increases far exceed other sectors. Non-ferrous metals, non-bank finance, national defense industry, and real estate mining led the rise in the 2005-2007 bull market. Computers, architectural decoration, and transportation led the rise in the 2014-2015 bull market. If this round of bull market is confirmed, technology, consumption, and non-bank finance will likely lead the rise in the whole market.
