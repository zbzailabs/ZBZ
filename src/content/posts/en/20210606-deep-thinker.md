### Weekly Thoughts

I am used to having free time every weekend to write something. Recently, I found that many times, facing the screen, I have no deep insights to write. Looking back at the records of the past six months, I found that the same problem is mostly just a few words, and many of them are forced to say sorrow for composing new lyrics.

Now, the pace of work and life is indeed fast. It is common to deal with 10 unrelated things a day. When free, people are lazy and do not want to think. When I have to write an article every week, my head is empty and I cannot write a truly deep article.

Since I started this blog and joined the ten-year agreement, my original intention was to be a high-quality, long-term personal blog, so I still have to produce some high-quality works. Next, I will try to collect thinking fragments of various topics, sharing by experts, and profound insights on a daily basis. I will choose a topic in each issue to discuss in depth and write some high-quality articles. I hope that many a little makes a mickle, and I can publish a collection after retirement.

When Franklin was young, he also used this method to improve his critical thinking ability. They selected a topic every week, completed articles independently, and gathered together to share and exchange ideas on weekends. Mapping to the present, it is similar to topics on Weibo or Douban.

Let's talk about "The Harm and Overcoming of Procrastination" next week.

At present, the land transfer fee distribution mechanism remains unchanged and is still local fiscal revenue. Therefore, this has basically no impact on the land market and housing prices.
