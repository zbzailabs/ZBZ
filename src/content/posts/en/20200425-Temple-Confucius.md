Qufu, the hometown of Confucius, is a famous historical and cultural city in China. The "Three Kongs" (Confucius Temple, Confucius Mansion, Confucius Cemetery) are World Cultural Heritage sites and famous tourist destinations.

**Confucius Temple**

The Confucius Temple is a place to offer sacrifices to Confucius. It was built in 478 BC, two years after the death of Confucius. After expansions and renovations in subsequent dynasties, it has become a magnificent architectural complex, known as the "Second Forbidden City".

**Confucius Mansion**

The Confucius Mansion is located next to the Confucius Temple and was the residence of the direct descendants of Confucius. It is the largest and best-preserved aristocratic mansion in China, displaying the living culture of the Confucius family.

**Confucius Cemetery**

The Confucius Cemetery is the cemetery of the Confucius family and his descendants. It is the largest, oldest, and best-preserved family cemetery in the world. There are more than 100,000 tombs and numerous ancient trees, creating a solemn and quiet atmosphere.
