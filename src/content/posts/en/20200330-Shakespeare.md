**Thoughts**

In the famous "Horse Manure Case," the judge ultimately awarded the manure to the plaintiff based on the case's impact on future generations and its influence on their behavioral norms. In the section "How to Resist a Mediocre Life," Teacher Luo Xiang mentioned that when we struggle to choose between reading erotica, listening to Guo Degang, and reading Shakespeare, three things that can equally bring happiness, we might consider: if the library could only leave one book for future generations, which one would you leave?

The two cases tell different stories, but the underlying logic is the same: our genes are encoded to reproduce and make future generations happier, wealthier, and nobler, but we are easily blinded by short-term interests and pleasures, often making wrong choices. If we use a longer-term vision and thinking to make choices, it will be much easier.
