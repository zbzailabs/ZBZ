**Thoughts**

When the epidemic causes panic, people tend to obtain more information to try to suppress panic, but obtaining information and deeply understanding information and making correct judgments are two different things. For example, seeing the news picture below about new infrastructure (infrastructure construction exerting force on the technology end), for most people, they can understand that new infrastructure involves seven major fields: 5G infrastructure, UHV, intercity rail transit, new energy vehicle charging piles, big data centers, artificial intelligence, and industrial Internet. However, if everyone is asked to choose only one field to buy in full and do short-term trading, I am afraid it will be a chaotic war among seven countries. But if we do a simple analysis, we can conclude: except for UHV and intercity rail transit, the other 5 are all themes that have been hyped in the early stage and prices are at normal points. In addition, there are not many targets regarding intercity rail transit in the market. Only UHV is in the price mismatch stage and is worth buying.

In addition, due to the lack of basic information screening and judgment ability, trading based on information itself will make traders anxious, and even forget some basic investment principles, but these principles are very important for selecting and holding targets. There are thousands of investment principles, but the ones to always remember are nothing more than the three basic principles given in Buffett's 2020 letter to shareholders: companies with good future cash flow, trustworthy management, and an appropriate price.

The first two points are easy to understand, but what is an appropriate price? Historical average price? Industry average PE, neither. The price that deviates from value is. Qualified investors should buy heavily when the market is inefficient in the short term and causes price deviations. When prices return to normal, quickly clear positions and leave. Usually, be like a crocodile, silently lurking underwater, attack quickly when prey appears, and then wait quietly again.
