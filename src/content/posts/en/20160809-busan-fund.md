I participated in the fund practitioner qualification exam on September 25th, and I felt like I was completely hollowed out.

I have seen every question, but I just didn't know which one to choose. In the first half of the laws and regulations exam, no one was absent. In the second half, half of the candidates were absent.

The exam site was at Zhejiang Highway Technician College.

A high-end vocational school logistics center, the exam room is very distinctive, get on the bus before getting on the machine, and ask the old driver to take you.

Although there was no participation of stars such as Huang Xiaoming, Hu Haiquan, and Quan Ling in April, which reduced a lot of entertainment, the exam was still very hot. Private equity big shots, handsome men and beautiful women in the financial industry gathered together, it was very lively.

The reason why so many people take the fund exam is that you can know by watching "Train to Busan", which will destroy the earth in minutes.

Last time, a number of private equity big shots complained that the fund exams were all about the secondary market and had nothing to do with private equity. This time, the Fund Association kindly added Subject 3 <Private Equity Exam>.

However, two private equity big shots' computers "lost contact with the server" during the exam, and the big shots left the scene angrily...

Apart from private equity big shots, who are the people who took the exam? Let's take a look at the data analysis from Baidu:

In terms of the geographical distribution of the national exam, Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou and Zhejiang are the main forces, which is consistent with the level of economic development.

Young people between the ages of 30 and 40 are the main force. Most of them are middle-level managers in companies or have just transferred to the financial industry from other industries. I communicated with the candidates on the left and right. One is from Ping An Securities, and the other is a small boss of a small loan company in Wenzhou.

In terms of gender ratio, big data is consistent with on-site observation results, with men and women each accounting for half.
