### Weekly Thoughts

In IPO audits, equity must penetrate to the ultimate beneficial natural person or SASAC. The regulatory agency's attention to the company's actual controller involves the essential issues of the company. How a company develops and whether it has investment value lies at the core in the company's actual controller. The will, judgment, and even personal living habits of the actual controller play an important decisive role in the company's value.

Industry, company, and actual controller are three important aspects of company due diligence. The first two can be judged correctly using standard methodologies, but judging the actual controller is difficult. First, it is difficult for ordinary investors to communicate face-to-face with the actual controller. Second, the actual controller's remarks are difficult to find in the media, and most of what is found is public relations articles. Third, investors themselves have a cognitive radius, and it is difficult to judge people.

---

I watched the "Camel Guest" trilogy this week, which mainly tells the story of the confrontation between bows and arrows and fast guns. In individual combat, bows and arrows are the only cold weapons that can duel with guns. Even now, crossbows similar to bows are often used as offensive weapons. In the martial arts of the Republic of China, knights often used bows and arrows to duel with the enemy's rifles. Another martial arts movie about bows and arrows in the Republic of China is "Arrow Scholar Liu Baiyuan".
