Sitting at the desk, facing the mountain of work on the to-do list, I felt that everything was important, but I didn't know where to start. Around 10 o'clock, colleagues, remote calls, emails, and WeChat came in one after another, dealing with various temporary things until noon. In the long run, output efficiency is getting lower and lower, performance is mediocre, unable to meet unit requirements, anxiety is getting heavier, sleep is getting worse, and quality of life is deteriorating. ## Principles of High Productivity

**Understand human nature and pursue fundamental meaning** Laziness is human nature. Without a strong driving force, it is difficult to accomplish great things, even a small thing. This driving force is the pursuit of the meaning of life. The meaning of life is definitely not fishing at work and sleeping after get off work. That is no different from a salted fish. The meaning of life does not lie in how big the ideal is or how small the dream is. Everyone's pursuit is different, but everyone should set the goal they pursue, not regret for wasting time, nor be ashamed of doing nothing.

**Time is money** The fingers are too wide, and time is too thin. It will flow through the fingers inadvertently. The importance of time cannot be overemphasized. If preaching is pale, you can price your own time, and you will know the value of time. Cherishing time is cherishing life.

**Set clear results and roadmaps for goals** In the 1960s, the United States launched a space war with the Soviet Union. Then US President John F. Kennedy condensed all space goals into one sentence "send a man to the moon and return safely before the end of the 1960s". The goal was clear and specific. Under the guidance of this goal, NASA formulated a very clear plan and roadmap. On July 21, 1969, Armstrong successfully landed on the moon. Later, when an emergency occurred during the [Apollo 13 moon landing](https:// .xyz/apollo13-okrs), the commander-in-chief of the moon landing, Gene, adopted the same strategy to clarify the goal for everyone: bring three astronauts back to Earth alive. Houston mobilized the entire project team to overcome difficulties such as hypoxia, carbon dioxide poisoning, orbital deviation, manual docking, etc., and Apollo 13 finally returned to Earth smoothly.

In order to comfort these people, a large number of entertainment activities are used to fill their lives to divert their attention and dissatisfaction and maintain social stability. **Choice is more important than hard work** In the fast-paced era, everyone has multiple roles, and the same role also faces different choices. ## Methods of High Productivity

**Maintain the best working state** Long working hours do not mean high work efficiency. It is necessary to arrange work and rest reasonably. A reasonable day schedule should be a three-three system, working 8 hours, sleeping 8 hours, and living daily 8 hours. Develop good work and rest habits, get up, work, rest, and sleep on time every day. A regular life will adjust the body to a stable biological clock, and you will be in the best state when working. There are exceptions. Sometimes you want to work very much, and you are not tired after working for 12 hours. Sometimes you don't want to work at all. This is normal. Do more when you want to work, and do less when you don't want to work.

**Prioritize things** No two tasks have the same priority. Before doing things, first carefully consider the to-do list, the attributes of each task, including importance, urgency, estimated time consumption, external collaboration required, etc., and make a choice. Task classification can be based on the four-quadrant rule, dividing work into four categories: important but not urgent, important and urgent, not important but not urgent, not important and urgent. Among them, the most important things should be arranged in the important but not urgent category as important things, leaving enough time to complete.

**Calculate the cost of everything** Time is the biggest opportunity cost. Even if a thing is important, it cannot let the work go on indefinitely. It is necessary to set deadlines and time consumption for all things. Must be completed within the specified period and duration.

**Set aside whole blocks of time** Important problems often require deep thinking. Deep thinking takes time. It takes a large period of time (starting from one hour) to think independently to consider comprehensively. It takes great concentration to be efficient. At this time, a time and space without interference is needed. Once the train of thought is interrupted, it takes a long time to recover.

**Do only one thing at a time** The brain is single-threaded when dealing with specific things. Only by concentrating and doing only one thing at a time can mistakes be avoided. Of course, in order to relieve fatigue, you can cut time into blocks, deal with one thing in a whole block of time, and deal with another thing in the next block of time, just like school classes.

**Pomodoro Technique** The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method founded by Francesco Cirillo in 1992. Set 25 minutes as a Pomodoro. Within a Pomodoro, cut off all possible disturbances, including the Internet, mobile phones, emails, calls, meetings, personnel interruptions, etc., and concentrate on work. After the Pomodoro ends, rest for 5 minutes, and then continue to the next Pomodoro. Rest for 15 minutes after completing 3 Pomodoros.

**Many a little makes a mickle** Large projects sometimes span years and months, such as writing a book, developing a game, etc. At this time, it is necessary to divide such tasks into reasonable small tasks. As long as small tasks are completed every day, you will get closer and closer to the big goal. Anyway, move first.

**Finish first, then perfect** Don't hope to do things well at once. Finish first, then seek perfection. Once you pursue perfection in the process of doing, you will distract your attention to details, such as typesetting while writing manuscripts, retrieving while writing, which will affect efficiency. Prepare a shorthand notebook to write down anything that distracts you, such as Google search terms, flashes of inspiration, new ideas, etc. If you write them down, it won't jump around anymore

**Good at using things** If other people can do a thing 80% well, let others do it and leave time for more important things. If a thing can be recombined from existing work results, don't reinvent the wheel. For example, program development should make good use of open source components, and fashion design should make good use of magazine clips. If a thing can be done or not, it can not be done.

**Make good use of inbox** When you enter the working state and focus on doing things, sometimes tasks will fly like paper. At this time, set up a task inbox to collect new tasks temporarily, and deal with new tasks after finishing the things at hand. Otherwise, a small episode will ruin a whole afternoon.

**Reserve maneuver time** Although there are 8 hours of working time every day, only 4~5 hours of work can be arranged, and 3~4 hours are set aside as emergency time to deal with emergencies, such as meetings, calls that have to be answered, etc. Maneuver time can also be used to deal with to-do items in the inbox, summarize the day's work, arrange tomorrow's plan, etc.

**Establish a reward mechanism** You can use games and entertainment as rewards after completing work to stimulate work interest.
