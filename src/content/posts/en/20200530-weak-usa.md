### Weekly Thoughts

- The editor-in-chief of Global Times is an anomaly, and his views are sometimes sharp. On May 28, when commenting on a series of recent operations by the United States, he said: The United States is a superpower, but it has been getting thinner from tossing around these years. It really hits the nail on the head.
- A person or an organization that shows its edge too much will inevitably attract blows from all directions. Ren Zhengfei and Huawei have been keeping a low profile, learning from American counterparts, and showing weakness to the US government, just to accumulate strength to charge towards the technological highlands and not expose themselves too early. Finally, coming to the 5G pass, the United States has no chance of winning in strangling Huawei.
- There are 600 million people in the country with a monthly income of less than 1,000 yuan, which is more shocking than the fact that 1 billion people in China have never been on a plane and 500 million people have never used a toilet. Deng Xiaoping's policy of "hide your strength and bide your time" must still be adhered to, and we must concentrate on doing our own things well.
- US oil rebounded by 60% this month, but VAL has not improved. Facing VAL and USO, why did I choose VAL and why did I not choose the leader? Is it related to personality, always wanting to settle for the next best thing, and just being okay is enough?
