### Weekly Thoughts

- Things have priorities. When there are many things to do, rely on task management to achieve goals, rather than working at will or passively. For related concepts, you can read "The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People", and for related methods and productivity tools in the digital age, you can refer to Computer Playthings.
- Choice is more important than effort, and waiting is more important than trading. Valaris (VAL), bought two months ago, has risen by 132%, with considerable returns.
- For orders of the same type, large and small, the time spent is the same. You should do large orders instead of small orders to be worthy of the time cost invested.
- Record working hours by the hour, and do only one thing at a time. Multi-threading is a lie, and single-threading is the efficient tool. If you don't believe it, you can try working while playing with your phone.
- Judging from the Nongfu Spring prospectus, Nongfu Spring is not a porter of nature, but a porter of plastic. All retail is essentially trade. Seize market trends and use commodity futures to hedge risks.

### Weekly Events, Period: 2020525-20200531

- Musk completed another feat, creating history for private enterprise manned aviation. Musk's greatness lies in the fact that he chose three entrepreneurial tracks that few people enter: new energy vehicles, aviation, and tunnels, and he has succeeded to a certain extent.
- Street stalls are hot, but the street stall economy will not be. No one will get rich by relying on street stalls, because street stalls are a crowded and threshold-free track. It is better to use the time spent setting up a street stall to learn and improve, and build a high career moat.
- Comparing Musk and street stall entrepreneurs, the return on investment is clear at a glance.
