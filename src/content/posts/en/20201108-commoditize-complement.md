### Weekly Thoughts

The business common sense behind the free code hosting platform Github and CRM system odoo is the Commoditize your Complement strategy. The complement strategy is a classic strategy for technology companies in the modern economic environment. Specifically, in the supply system, while quickly occupying the market share of its own business, it minimizes the premium of upstream and downstream links as much as possible, so as to more effectively obtain the surplus value of users for its own products and increase exposure to potential users.

In 2002, former Microsoft product manager Joel Spolsky discussed the phenomenon of complement strategy being particularly obvious in technology companies, especially software/service companies, in his article Strategy Letter V: The Economics of Open Source.

Any product, service or software in the market actually has corresponding Substitutes and Complements.

- Substitutes refer to other products that users consider when they feel your product is too expensive. For example, pork is a substitute for beef. If you sell pork, when the price of beef rises, many users will buy pork instead, and your sales will rise.
- Complements refer to mutually complementary products purchased with your product. For example, gasoline is a complement to cars. A typical example is that operating systems and computer hardware are also complements. When the price of computers drops, the sales of operating systems will rise.

For companies, the main strategic direction is to reduce the price of complements corresponding to their core products as much as possible. If you have the ability to reduce the price of complements, you will be able to gain more sales and revenue and enjoy the exposure and popularity brought by the expansion of the audience of complements.

When IBM designed the PC system architecture, it chose a large number of standardized accessories and detailed the specifications and interface standards between various accessories in the legendary IBM-PC Technical Reference Manual. In this way, other suppliers can participate in the design and production of PC accessories very easily. The result is that a large number of low-priced memory sticks, hard drives, graphics cards and other accessories flooded the market, greatly enriching the PC accessories market. For IBM, their goal is to expand the accessories market, because accessories and PCs are complements, and low-priced accessories will greatly promote PC sales.
Microsoft used the same trick. When Microsoft initially licensed the PC-DOS operating system to IBM, it dispelled IBM's request for exclusive licensing through negotiations. Microsoft's move was intended to eliminate the premium between PCs and expand the PC market. We know the story afterwards clearly. The price of PCs continued to fall and began to be accepted by more and more users. The Windows operating system gradually became the most popular operating system in the world, and Microsoft's valuation took off all the way until it became the most valuable company in the world.

GitHub was acquired by Microsoft, and odoo cooperated with Inspur Information. The trend of cooperation between SaaS and PaaS products and basic cloud has become more and more obvious. SaaS needs to rely on the ecological resources of basic cloud service providers to expand its product possibilities, and basic cloud service providers need SaaS products as carriers to provide developers and R&D teams with more convenient computing services.
